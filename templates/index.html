References for 17th century paints

The pigment checker is an attempt to create a reference collection of 17th century Dutch paint samples by the Technical Art History master's at the University of Amsterdam.

Where possible, the paints have been created according to the methods described by painting treatises of the time. Pigments were mixed with as little linseed oil as possible using a muller to create a buttery smooth paint.

Colour

The painted squares can be used to examine the colour that specific paints produce when mixed with linseed oil.

Hiding power

To demonstrate the hiding power of each paint, three different types of underdrawing are shown as black lines. Each pigment checker square is painted lightly on the left and thicker on the right.

Mixing

Lead white was often mixed with other colours to create tints from paints. To show the effects of lead white mixing, every paint has been mixed with 50% v/v lead white.
